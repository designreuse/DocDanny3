ABOUT DR. DANNY BENMOSHE

Dr. Benmoshe is a board certified Diplomate of the American Academy of Psychiatry and Neurology. He is dedicated to the diagnosis and treatment of all neurological conditions involving both the central and peripheral nervous systems.

His fellowship training in Neurophysiology enables him to perform specific diagnostic testing including electro-encephalograms (EEGs) and electromyograms/nerve conduction studies (EMG/NCVs) for the diagnosis of seizures and nerve/muscle disorders respectively.

Dr. Benmoshe also has additional interest and training in the treatment of headaches and movement disorders such as Parkinson’s. He uses a holistic approach to treatment that includes pharmacotherapy, physical therapy, Botox injections (for migraines) and diet and lifestyle changes. Furthermore, Dr. Benmoshe’s integrated method focuses on the mind and body connection that is affected in various neurological conditions.

Dr. Benmoshe’s dedication to the field of neurology began during his time as an undergraduate student at UCLA while taking his neuroscience courses. It is during this time that his exposure to the study of the brain/mind has made him inspired in pursuing a career as a neurologist. He is dedicated to his continuing medical education and attends conferences around the country in his field.

Dr. Benmoshe received his training:

  • Undergraduate Education: UCLA, Bachelors of Science in Neurosciences

  • Medical School: Albert Einstein College of Medicine

  • Internship: Harbor-UCLA Medical Center Medicine/Surgery department

  • Residency: Chief Resident, Harbor-UCLA Medical Center Department of Neurology

  • Fellowship: Neurophysiology, Harbor-UCLA Medical Center Department of Neurology

Volunteer:

  • Lestonnac Free Clinic Los Alamitos faculty neurologist


In his free time, Dr. Benmoshe enjoys cycling, cardio-fitness, Pilates, yoga and meditation.
